What you'll make

You'll make a multipage website using the Webflow Editor.

It'll have the following features:

  • A second page
  • A reusable Header symbol
  • A reusable Footer symbol
  • A reusable Navigation symbol

What you'll learn

  • How to create elements of a web page using Webflow's Editor.

What you'll need

  • A topic to create your site about
  • A Webflow account

New Page

  1. Login to Webflow
  2. Open your Portfolio site
  3. Click Pages, then New Page, and name it Practice Projects

Header symbol

  1. Click Pages and return to your Home page
  2. Right-click on your Header and turn it into a Symbol with the name Header
  3. Click Pages and go to your Practice Projects page
  4. Click Symbols and drag your Header symbol onto the page

Footer symbol

  1. Apply steps 4 to 7 again to do the same with your Footer
  2. Add a blank section to break up your Header and Footer

Publish point

You've added a complete feature to your website. You should Publish it.

Navigation Section

  1. Click Add and drag a new Section below your Header
  2. Change the tag to Nav

Home button

  1. Click Add and drag a Link Block into your Nav Section
  2. Change it to link to a Page, and selection your Home page
  3. Add text to your Link Block to label it as Home

Practice Projects button

  1. Apply steps 3 to 5 again to do the same with your Practice Projects button

Test

  1. Preview your website and check that your link buttons work.

Publish point

Spacing

  1. Add Padding space on the left and right side of your Nav Section
  2. Add Padding space on all sides of both of your Link Blocks

Colours

  1. Change the Background Colour to something you prefer
  2. Click one of your Link Blocks and choose the All Links selector from the list of selectors
  3. Remove the underline and change the Colour to something you prefer

Navigation Symbol

  1. Right-click on your Navigation and turn it into a Symbol with the name Navigation
  2. Click Pages and go to your Practice Projects page
  3. Click Symbols and drag your Navigation symbol onto the page

Publish point
